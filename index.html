AMReX at Exascale

AMReX at Exascale is a technical workshop organized by the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology at Cambridge on 24 - 25 June 2024 to coincide with the annual GRTL collaboration meeting (June 26-27).

More

About

GRTeclyn is our AMReX based AMR solver for Einstein's equations of General Relativity designed for exascale computing by offloading our calculations to GPU accelerated systems. The University of Cambridge currently hosts the Dawn supercomputer - based on the new Intel GPU Max series, this is the largest AI supercomputer in the UK. We're interested in all things AMReX and GPU based: optimizing AMR methods for exascale computing, GPU acceleration with SYCL, and in-situ visualization.

Centre for Theoretical Cosmology

Since the first COSMOS supercomputer in 2012(?) commissioned by Prof Stephen Hawking, the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology (CTC) has been dedicated to the advance of high performance computing for research into the origins and structure of our Universe and is a designated Intel OneAPI Centre for Excellence. The CTC is additionally funded by the ExCALIBUR programme to explore the potential for exascale computing in the UK.
